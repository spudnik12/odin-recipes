Scotch Eggs

  • Description
  • Ingredients
  • How to cook

Description

Perfectly made hardboiled eggs dressed in savory sausage goodness makes a great breakfast item to enjoy at home or on the road. Pairing with dijon dipping sauce, allows for a strong spicy flavor that balances out the savory flavor profile.

Ingredients

  • Hardboiled Eggs, qty: 8
    • Either purchased, or preferrably made.
  • Uncooked Eggs, qty: 4
    • Eggwash bath for bread crumbs to stick to hardboiled eggs.
  • Panko Bread Crumbs, 1 1/2 cups
    • Provides crunchy exterior.
  • Italian Sausage, 2 lbs
    • Savory meaty exterior to the hardboiled eggs.
  • Flour, 1/2 cup
    • Dries hardboiled egg and allows for sausage meat to adhere.
  • Vegetable Oil, 1 cup
    • Frying medium for coated eggs.
  • Dijon mustard
    • Dipping sauce that adds spicy flavor to the egg.

Steps

  1. In a pan, heat the vegetable oil to about 350 degrees Fahreinheit

  2. Prepare the hardboiled eggs

    • Bring a pot of water to boil.
    • Drop eggs in for approximately 4 minutes.
    • Take out eggs immediately and chill in an ice-bath.
    • Chill overnight. Peel when ready.

  3. Prepare with panko-sausage bread mix

    • Beat an egg; set aside in bowl.
    • Take sausage, squeeze out from sleeves.
    • Lightly wet the peeled-hardboiled eggs. Shake excess water. Roll in flour, lightly coating it.
    • Wrap sausage around the egg, completely covering it in a thin layer.
    • Dip sausaged-covered-hardboiled egg into the egg wash. The roll into panko bread crumbs.
    • Cook each egg in the hot cooking oil about 4-5 minutes. If not completely submerged, periodically turn over until entire egg exterior is completely brown.

  4. Set aside some dijon dipping sauce and enjoy.
